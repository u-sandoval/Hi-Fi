Data Privacy

Ethical

  • Not sharing personal data in prompts
  • Using anonymized examples

Unethical

  • Entering real names, SSNs, or passwords
  • Sharing sensitive university data

Academic Honesty

Ethical

  • Using ChatGPT to brainstorm or clarify topics
  • Citing AI-generated ideas when used

Unethical

  • Copying and submitting ChatGPT output directly
  • Using it to complete take-home exams

Irresponsible Use of AI

Misusing AI can lead to serious legal, professional, and ethical consequences. Below are two real-world examples of how AI misuse has caused significant issues:

  • Wadsworth v. Walmart Inc. & Jetson Electric Bikes, LLC

    In early 2025, a federal judge found that three lawyers from Morgan & Morgan tried to back up their arguments with made-up cases that were 100% generated by AI. Because they presented these fake citations as real, the judge punished them: one lawyer lost his permission to practice in Wyoming and was fined $3,000, and his two colleagues each paid $1,000. This case highlights how misuse of AI—especially creating false “evidence”—can lead to serious professional and ethical consequences.

  • Kohls et al. v. Ellison

    In January 2025, Minnesota lawmakers tried to block a new “anti-deepfake” law that makes it illegal to upload AI-manipulated videos without clear labels. The court said no—because parody deepfakes count as protected speech and the challengers waited too long to object. The court also slapped down the state for trying to fix its expert testimony by slipping in more AI-generated fake citations after the fact. This ruling spotlights the dangers when AI is used unethically to manipulate evidence and influence legal outcomes.
